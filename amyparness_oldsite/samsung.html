Samsung e-commerce

Business Goals

Innovation. Samsung's marketing department in Korea was working with a large agency in the USA and wanted a UX expert to have a second look at the work and present ideas.

Challenges

Creating a best in class e-commerce site that made it easy to find products, product support and important product details. The ability to compare items that seem very similar to customers, but have unique features.

Solutions

Restructure the information architecture. Rearrange the stacking order of information on the landing pages. Change the way Samsung presents itself on the Homepage. Innovative compare features for TVs and mobile. Create a TV buying guide to help people pick the right size and technology.

Role

Presenting solutions to stakeholders in Korea through translators. Directing a team made up of a UX designer and an Art Director. Creating a content strategy. Restructuring the IA.

Research and Process

  • Competitive research
  • Site experience surveys
  • Site analysis tools
  • VOC tool
  • User journey
  • Content strategy
  • SEO recommendations
  • Design workshops
  • Wireframes
  • Creative direction

Content strategy

The homepage needed to show top products and cement Samsung as an innovator in the tech industry. We used a mix of product promos focusing on their technical differentiators in the marketplace and innovation coming out of the Samsung Labs.

We recommended a TV guide which was implemented.

Shoppers & Owners: PDP

Most people go to Samsung to learn more about new products or get support for products they already own. During the research phase, we saw customers had trouble finding things like products, documentation and could not tell products apart. We saw opportunities to make the product details pages more visual in place of text details or scrolling all the way down the page. We changed the product photos to show a side view, what's in the box and back ports. Top features would have icons at the top of the page.

Support in the PDP

Support was incorporated into the product pages instead of having the customer click away from the product page. We added product specific support documents, view technical specs and support guides and the ability to contact support Based on search results and customer service reports, the top requests from customers around support were provided instead of having to download a file from a support section.

Information architecture

We presented two different options for the main navigation of the large site. We changed the IA overall using heat maps and site data as our guides. The footer was redesigned for SEO and became a secondary navigation for top categories.

New features

We presented several ideas of how they could innovate. The shopping cart allowed customers to save things to the cart without adding them into the actual cart to view cost of adding compatible products. We also recommend sets like "Home Theater" that came bundles with a TV and sound system.

We presented a compare feature for TVs and mobile since these are products that have similar models and customers need help choosing the right model. This was implemented on Samsung.com.

Final designs

Samsung was looking to elevate the brand. We did this with a fresh color scheme and more white space.
