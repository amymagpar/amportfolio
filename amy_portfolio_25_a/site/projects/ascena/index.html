Multi-brand Design Systems

design system e-commerce omnichannel

Designing and implementing a multi-brand accessible design system unified the brand identities and expanded the user base while ensuring consistency and efficiency across platforms. The new system streamlined collaboration across product, marketing, and engineering teams, reduced design redundancies by 55%, and accelerated feature rollouts by 30%, improving customer satisfaction and saving budgets.

Business Goals

Replatform two highly trafficked sites with the least impact. Create user-friendly ADA-compliant responsive designs for both e-commerce sites.

Challenges

Two very large e-commerce sites needed to be redesigned to make them more modern and user-friendly without losing much revenue. How to prioritize what to improve and what can wait for post launch?

Solutions

Used a LeanUX process to prioritize designs. My team worked directly with the off-shore dev team and business-side PMs in order to focus tasks and improve the process. UX took a collaborative role instead of just delivering every few weeks. Created an ADA compliant Design System.

Role

UX Manager- Design management governance. Managed a team of UX/UI designers and off shore front-end development. Worked collaboratively with Product Managers.

Design system and components

A Design System and components had to be created for both sites with detailed annotations in order to allow off-shore dev teams to build using the correct spacing and design. Using components also allowed us to redesign for ADA compliance by changing colors and spacing quickly throughout all of the pages.

ADA compliance

A thorough ADA review of the new designs showed that we needed to inmytheme requirements into our new Design System. Things like contrast, using the same icons for different actions, and outlining colors for products were some of the changes we made.
