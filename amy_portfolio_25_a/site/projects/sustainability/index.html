Cisco Systems

Scaling Sustainability

sustainability user journeys design collaboration

As the UX Lead of the Global Sustainability Messaging Tiger Team at Cisco Meraki, I spearheaded the development of a compelling sustainability narrative designed to engage and inform our customers.

By working with the ESO and CSO teams and studying customer research, we crafted a powerful story that highlights sustainability throughout the user journey, real-world impact stories, and future goals. I designed interactive elements, and clickable infographics to create an engaging and immersive experience.

Business Goals

Improve sustainability messaging and program awareness. Many RFPs to sales were requiring power and other information in order to choose products to meet government requirements and company commitments.

My goals

Give customers the information they need to chose more sustainable products and solutions to build energy efficient data centers and inform them on proper disposal of End of Life products.

Challenges

Multiple departments and legal were required to approve all messaging.

Solutions

Show, don’t tell approach. Focus on what customers care about like energy management and power consumption. Place this information within different user journeys to tell a story.

Role

As UX Lead on the Global Sustainability Tiger team. Represent UX in sustainability efforts across Cisco's diverse product range. Led cross-functional teams in redesigning the content strategy based on actionable insights from data analysis. 

A lack of requirements call for actionable research.

  • Spearheaded a comprehensive data analysis project to review user journeys of sustainability-focused users.
  • Directed the analysis of UX research data, including interviews, surveys, and usability testing, to gain deep insights into user preferences and behavior.

Results:

  • Identified critical barriers, such as fragmented information placements and the absence of energy data, which hindered user engagement.
  • Recognized that high-level sustainability information, while engaging for Press and people researching Cisco, failed to drive users to product and solution pages, necessitating a strategic overhaul.
Opportunity 1

Show, Don't Tell

Sustainable solutions were transformed into key features in the sales funnel.

The visibility of product and solutions' energy certifications and power management capabilities were highlighted.

Opportunity 2

Consistency

Energy certifications and data points were integrated into product pages, marketing materials, and user interfaces to reinforce this information throughout the user journey.

Critical sustainability information was transformed from being buried to prominent and easily accessible.

Opportunity 3

Design & Best Practices

Cisco can create data center design guides and best practice guides can empower Cisco's customers to make informed decisions and take actionable steps toward building more sustainable data centers and network infrastructures.

Elevating sustainability through design system innovation

To support Cisco's environmental commitments, RFPs, and advance the circular economy model, I developed two scalable patterns that seamlessly integrated sustainability messaging across multiple user touchpoints.

These enhancements accommodate varying content levels, from one to six key sustainability facts, ensuring flexibility for product pages, end-of-life notices, and marketing collateral. By aligning with the broader design system framework and working with the design system team, the patterns maintain consistency within the system while amplifying environmental storytelling.

Thought Leadership

In order to measure power accurately, standardization is key. Several white papers were released to support the our efforts to accurately measure product power. Cisco is leading these standardization efforts across the information and communications technology (ICT) industry.

Impact

Pioneered the development and implementation of a pattern to effectively promote lifecycle programs for hardware products and end-of-life information.
Established a visually distinctive, scalable pattern for consistent sustainability messaging across the site.
Integrated certifications Energy Star and Titanium into the redesigned content, ensuring they were prominently featured.
Significantly improved the user experience by making critical sustainability information easily accessible, engaging, and seamlessly integrated into the user journey.
