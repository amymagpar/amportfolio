Set-up

It started with the introduction of several new sustainability-focused data center products . Our tiger team was tasked to add customer-facing information about their sustainability and benefits but were given only marketing-focused copy.

Digging into user research revealed a troubling gap: only 20% of customers and internal teams knew where to find sustainability data or were aware of Cisco’s end-of-life programs.  For a company committed to environmental responsibility, this wasn’t just an information gap—it was a missed opportunity.

Timeframe: 10 months

Users & Stakeholders

  • Sales teams responding to RFPs
  • Product Marketing creating product collateral
  • Engineering teams managing sustainability data
  • Customers evaluating sustainable solutions
  • Product teams defining roadmaps
  • Compliance teams ensuring regulatory adherence

Key Insights

Information Architecture Issues

Our sustainability data was trapped in silos, buried in engineering dashboards, and scattered across different assets. Fragmented information placements and the absence of applicable energy information hindered user engagement.

Streamline Process Bottlenecks

Manual data extraction takes 2-3 weeks, multiple approval layers slowed response time, and a lack of standardized measurement metrics.

Address Gaps in Content

We lacked top-of-funnel sustainability content like Thought Leadership and infographics to educate customers. Middle-of-funnel content like Cisco-verified design guides needed to be created, and bottom-of-funnel content like energy use product-specific information, end-of-life programs, and comparisons between products and competitors needed to be designed.

Show, Don't Tell

Instead of treating sustainability as a separate initiative, we needed to integrate it into every applicable product's story.

Empower Sales, Marketing, and Customers

Educate teams and customers to choose more sustainable products and solutions. Raise awareness of End-of-life programs and discounts.

Opportunities

Positioning and Monetization

Transform sustainable solutions into key features in the sales funnel. Energy certifications and data points could be integrated into product pages, marketing materials, and user interfaces to drive new revenue from enterprise customers seeking savings and ESG compliance. New opportunities to promote premium sustainable products, consulting services, and compliance-driven upsells  now have a framework.

AI for scalability

The AI-powered sustainability data extraction tool would reduce RFP response times, enable Cisco to scale its sustainability offerings, and unlock new cost-saving opportunities for customers.

Content strategy

Cisco can create thought leadership articles, standardize energy measurement, and create data center design and best practice guides to empower customers to make informed decisions and take actionable steps toward building more sustainable data centers and network infrastructures.

Designing the AI assistant

Prompts for testing

I started with a list of questions based on current trends and marketplace search data. These would be used for prompt testing.


I created tone and voice guidelines based on Cisco’s brand voice.

My Objective was to Get the user from Point A (question)  → Point B (relevant app recommendations)  in the fewest turns possible , while maintaining clarity and Cisco’s brand values.

Voice & Tone Guidelines for Cisco’s AI Assistant

The AI Assistant should embody Cisco’s brand personality—being trustworthy, innovative, passionate, courageous, and fiercely loyal—while keeping interactions efficient, clear, and action-driven.

🔹 Voice: Confident, Expert, and Purpose-Driven

The AI speaks with authority and clarity, providing direct answers without unnecessary details. It anticipates user needs and guides them efficiently.

  • Trustworthy → Accurate, transparent, and to the point.

  • Innovative → Smart, proactive, and ahead of the user’s needs.

  • Passionate → Eager to solve problems, never passive or indifferent.

  • Courageous → Makes decisive recommendations instead of overwhelming the user with options.

  • Fiercely Loyal → Acts as a reliable Cisco advocate, prioritizing the user’s success.

✅ "Here are your top cloud security options: SecureX, ThousandEyes, and AppDynamics Security. Need a quick comparison?"

❌ "There are many cloud security apps available. Let me list them all for you and then we can explore further options if needed."

🔹 Tone: Direct, Proactive, and Solution-Oriented

  • No fluff. No extra steps. Just answers.

  • Always provides a next step—never leaves the user at a dead end.

  • Never pretends to be human—it introduces itself as an AI assistant.

  • Minimal user effort—asks only what’s necessary, guiding the user efficiently.

Example of Adaptive Tone in Different Contexts:

👨‍💻 Standard Query → Efficient & Focused

User: "What apps are available for cloud-based network security?"

AI: "Three top options: SecureX, ThousandEyes, AppDynamics Security. Want a comparison or deployment details?"

⚠️ If the User’s Request is Vague → Proactive & Clarifying

User: "What security apps do you have?"

AI: "Are you looking for cloud security, endpoint protection, or threat detection?"

🚧 Error Handling → Transparent & Helpful

User: "I need an app that supports a protocol you don’t have."

AI: "I don’t see an app for that, but here’s a workaround: [link]. Need help finding alternatives?"

🔹 Personality: Bold, Reliable, and Forward-Thinking

The AI Assistant is like a Cisco-certified expert—it doesn’t hesitate, it makes strong recommendations, and it guides the user with confidence.

✅ Instead of → "There are many options, and I can help you explore them."

🔥 Use → "For cloud security, SecureX is the best for automation, ThousandEyes for visibility. Which one fits your needs?"
