A lack of requirements call for actionable research.

  • Spearheaded a comprehensive data analysis project to review user journeys of sustainability-focused users.
  • Directed the analysis of UX research data, including interviews, surveys, and usability testing, to gain deep insights into user preferences and behavior.

Results:

  • Identified critical barriers, such as fragmented information placements and the absence of energy data, which hindered user engagement.
  • Recognized that high-level sustainability information, while engaging for Press and people researching Cisco, failed to drive users to product and solution pages, necessitating a strategic overhaul.
Opportunity 1

Show, Don't Tell

Sustainable solutions were transformed into key features in the sales funnel.

The visibility of product and solutions' energy certifications and power management capabilities were highlighted.

Opportunity 2

Consistency

Energy certifications and data points were integrated into product pages, marketing materials, and user interfaces to reinforce this information throughout the user journey.

Critical sustainability information was transformed from being buried to prominent and easily accessible.

Opportunity 3

Design & Best Practices

Cisco can create data center design guides and best practice guides can empower Cisco's customers to make informed decisions and take actionable steps toward building more sustainable data centers and network infrastructures.

Scalable Pattern Integration

A lifecycle program pattern I designed is being used on hardware product pages and in end-of-life notices to raise awareness of Cisco's commitments to the environment and a circular economy model.

Recognizing the need for a versatile approach, I implemented a scalable pattern that could accommodate between one and six key sustainability facts. This pattern was seamlessly integrated into various touch points, from product pages to marketing collateral, ensuring a cohesive user experience.

By working with the design team, I created visually distinct elements that highlighted sustainability features, which led to a more informed and empowered customer base. The scalable nature of this pattern allowed for consistent application across different product lines without compromising user experience.

Impact

Pioneered the development and implementation of a pattern to effectively promote lifecycle programs for hardware products and end-of-life information.
Established a visually distinctive, scalable pattern for consistent sustainability messaging across the site.
Integrated certifications Energy Star and Titanium into the redesigned content, ensuring they were prominently featured.
Significantly improved the user experience by making critical sustainability information easily accessible, engaging, and seamlessly integrated into the user journey.
