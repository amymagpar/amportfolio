Using an AI POC, I could decrease RFP response times from weeks to days or hours, customer awareness of end-of-life programs rose from 20% to 75%, and sustainability content engagement increased by 200%.

The initiative demonstrated how strategic UX leadership could turn sustainability from a compliance requirement into a competitive advantage by making environmental impact data visible and accessible at every key decision point.

A blind spot in awareness and a sales team in crisis

It started with the introduction of several new sustainability-focused data center products. Our tiger team was tasked to add customer-facing information about their sustainability and benefits but were given only marketing-focused copy.

Digging into user research revealed a troubling gap: only 20% of customers and internal teams knew where to find sustainability data or were aware of Cisco’s end-of-life programs. For a company committed to environmental responsibility, this wasn’t just an information gap—it was a missed opportunity for impact.

My Goals

Make Cisco a leader in power reporting & sustainable networking. Help the team visualize how sustainability becomes a core strategy.

AI for Pain Point Reduction

The product dashboard needed to be overhauled to be used by other teams to extract power and material information. In lieu of a redesign, I created an AI assistant proof of concept to help teams quickly extract and format sustainability data from the complex product database. As one product manager noted, "What used to take weeks now could take minutes."

Using Metrics to Drive Design

During stakeholder interviews, a product engineering sustainability manager shared a story that crystallized our challenge:

"I have two RFPs from sales due in 48 hours requiring power usage metrics for our networking solutions. I spent a day just trying to find if we have this data, only to discover it would take weeks to compile. We will lose the bids.”

This wasn't an isolated incident. I directed the analysis of UX research data, including interviews, surveys, and usability testing, to gain deep insights into user preferences and behavior. Our research revealed:

  • Marketing teams were surfacing generic sustainability content because they did not have product-specific data
  • Engineers had some of the data in a hard-to-use dashboard.
  • Sales teams were missing opportunities because they couldn't quickly access energy or manufacturing information.
  • Customers were making purchasing decisions without awareness of more lustainable options.

The Lesson

The key insight wasn't just about making data accessible—it was about making sustainability visible at every touchpoint where it mattered. By integrating environmental impact information into existing workflows and user journeys, we turned sustainability from an afterthought into a competitive differentiator.

This project taught us that sometimes the most impactful UX solutions aren't about creating new features—they're about surfacing hidden value in ways that naturally align with user needs.

Elevating sustainability through design system innovation

To support Cisco's environmental commitments, RFPs, and advance the circular economy model, I developed two scalable patterns that seamlessly integrated sustainability messaging across multiple user touchpoints.

These enhancements accommodate varying content levels, from one to six key sustainability facts, ensuring flexibility for product pages, end-of-life notices, and marketing collateral. By aligning with the broader design system framework and working with the design system team, the patterns maintain consistency within the system while amplifying environmental storytelling.
