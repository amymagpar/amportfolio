Set-up
It started with the introduction of several new sustainability-focused data center products. Our tiger team was tasked to add customer-facing information about their sustainability and benefits but were given only marketing-focused copy.
Digging into user research revealed a troubling gap: only 20% of customers and internal teams knew where to find sustainability data or were aware of Cisco’s end-of-life programs. For a company committed to environmental responsibility, this wasn’t just an information gap—it was a missed opportunity.
Timeframe: 10 months

Users & Stakeholders

  • Sales teams responding to RFPs
  • Product Marketing creating product collateral
  • Engineering teams managing sustainability data
  • Customers evaluating sustainable solutions
  • Product teams defining roadmaps
  • Compliance teams ensuring regulatory adherence

Cohort analysis, research, and tracking

I created a sustainability cohort in our analytics tools to analyze user journeys and opportunities. Working with UXR, I analyzed previous user interviews and determined subjects for research at upcoming Cisco Live events.

Key Insights

Information Architecture Issues
Our sustainability data was trapped in silos, buried in engineering dashboards, and scattered across different assets. Fragmented information placements and the absence of applicable energy information hindered user engagement.
Streamline Process Bottlenecks
Manual data extraction takes 2-3 weeks, multiple approval layers slowed response time, and a lack of standardized measurement metrics.
Address Gaps in Content
We lacked top-of-funnel sustainability content like Thought Leadership and infographics to educate customers. Middle-of-funnel content like Cisco-verified design guides needed to be created, and bottom-of-funnel content like energy use product-specific information, end-of-life programs, and comparisons between products and competitors needed to be designed.
Show, Don’t Tell
Instead of treating sustainability as a separate initiative, we needed to integrate it into every applicable product’s story.
Empower Sales, Marketing, and Customers
Educate teams and customers to choose more sustainable products and solutions. Raise awareness of End-of-life programs and discounts.

Opportunities

Positioning and Monetization
Transform sustainable solutions into key features in the sales funnel. Energy certifications and data points could be integrated into product pages, marketing materials, and user interfaces to drive new revenue from enterprise customers seeking savings and ESG compliance. New opportunities to promote premium sustainable products, consulting services, and compliance-driven upsells now have a framework.
AI for scalability
The AI-powered sustainability data extraction tool would reduce RFP response times, enable Cisco to scale its sustainability offerings, and unlock new cost-saving opportunities for customers.
Content strategy
Cisco can create thought leadership articles, standardize energy measurement, and create data center design and best practice guides to empower customers to make informed decisions and take actionable steps toward building more sustainable data centers and network infrastructures.

AI for Scalability

During stakeholder interviews, a product engineering sustainability manager shared a story that crystallized our challenge:
“I have two RFPs from sales due in 48 hours requiring power usage metrics for our networking solutions. I spent a day trying to find if we have this data, only to discover it would take weeks to compile. We will lose the bids.”
A product dashboard existed for engineers and industrial designers to input attributes from manufacturing and testing while designing products. The dashboard has the potential to be leveraged by other teams to surface the customer-facing sustainability information needed to make better product decisions and guide purchases. In place of a redesign, I created an AI proof of concept to help teams extract and format sustainability data from the complex product database.
As one product manager noted,
“What used to take weeks now could take minutes.”

Designing modules for the detailed product dashboard

Being able to compare products was a key need for sales, customers, and partners. Guiding sales and marketing to promote more efficient products and product teams could see areas for innovation.

Beginning to map the AI tool for engineering

Viewing the dashboard as a tool for outside teams, and a single source of truth for sign-offs from legal and brand. I did the intent modeling and flow diagram for the conversational AI tool to review with engineering.

Positioning and Visibility

Customers need energy use, certification, and management information to choose the best products. Critical sustainability information was transformed from being buried to a prominent competitive advantage.

Focus areas

Prominent placement of energy certifications, end-of-life program information, and clear visibility into power management capabilities were my key goals.
I designed interactive elements, and clickable infographics to create an engaging and immersive experience.

New components

Working with the design systems team, I designed scalable patterns that could showcase sustainability information within existing user journeys.

Highlighting our programs

Standardized iconography with existing Cisco design language was used to showcase lifecycle programs.

Applicable sustainability data in the product datasheets

As the UX lead for the datasheet initiative, I gained insights into the data that customers find valuable when researching, upgrading, and installing products. This understanding enabled our team to effectively promote sustainable products and platforms by giving customers applicable information.

Thought Leadership

To measure power accurately, standardization is key. Several white papers were released to support our efforts to measure product power accurately. Cisco is leading these standardization efforts across the information and communications technology (ICT) industry.
Marketing sustainability with visuals

Outcomes & Learnings

The key insight wasn’t just about making data accessible—it was about making sustainability visible at every touchpoint where it mattered. By integrating environmental impact information into existing workflows and user journeys, we turned sustainability from an afterthought into a competitive differentiator.
This project taught us that sometimes the most impactful UX solutions aren’t about creating new features—they’re about surfacing hidden value in ways that naturally align with user needs.
