Data-driven: Cohort creation, analysis, research, and tracking

The first step is always research and data analysis. Working with UXR, I analyzed previous user interviews and determined subjects for research at upcoming Cisco Live events. Our team interviewed stakeholders in 6 departments to gain deep insights into internal process, customer preferences and behavior.

During stakeholder interviews, a product engineering sustainability manager shared a story that crystallized our challenge:

"I have two RFPs from sales due in 48 hours requiring power usage metrics for our networking solutions. I spent a day just trying to find if we have this data, only to discover it would take weeks to compile. We will lose the bids.”

Data-Driven Growth Architecture

Sustainability funnels were not being tracked. I created a sustainability cohort in our analytics tools to analyze user journeys and opportunities.

Key Insights

  1. Information Architecture Issues

    Our sustainability data was trapped in silos, buried in engineering dashboards, and scattered across different assets. Fragmented information placements and the absence of applicable energy information hindered user engagement and confidence.

  2. Streamline Process Bottlenecks

    Manual data extraction takes 2-3 weeks,and has multiple approval layers that slow response time. There was a lack of standardized sustainability metrics to compare or promote products properly.

  3. Address Gaps in Content

    Customers need energy use, certification, and management information to choose the best products.

    We lacked top-of-funnel sustainability content like Thought Leadership and infographics to educate customers. Middle-of-funnel content like Cisco-verified design guides needed to be created, and bottom-of-funnel content like product-specific energy use information and comparisons between products and competitors.

  4. Show, Don't Tell

    Instead of treating sustainability as a separate initiative, we needed to integrate it into every applicable product's story- dashboards, product pages, datatsheets, etc..

  5. Empower Sales, Marketing, and Customers

    Educate teams and customers to choose more sustainable products and solutions. Raise awareness of lifecycle programs and discounts.

AI for Scalability

A product dashboard existed for engineers and industrial designers to input attributes from manufacturing and testing while designing products. The dashboard has the potential to be leveraged by other teams to surface the customer-facing sustainability information needed to make better product decisions and guide purchases.

We lacked proper funding to redesign the dashboard. To support our teams, I created an AI proof of concept to extract and format sustainability data from the complex product database. I redesigned the dashboard using the product design system.

As one product manager noted:

"What used to take weeks now could take minutes."

Outcomes & Learnings

The key insight wasn't just about making data accessible—it was about making sustainability visible at every touchpoint where it mattered. By integrating environmental impact information into existing workflows and user journeys, we turned sustainability from an afterthought into a competitive differentiator.

This project taught us that sometimes the most impactful UX solutions aren't about creating new features—they're about surfacing hidden value in ways that naturally align with user needs.
